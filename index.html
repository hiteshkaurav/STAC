Fundamental Analysis Methodology

Key Metrics Used

  • ROE (Return on Equity): Measures profitability relative to shareholder equity
  • ROCE (Return on Capital Employed): Indicates efficiency of capital utilization
  • PE Ratio: Price-to-earnings valuation metric
  • Debt-to-Equity: Financial leverage and risk assessment
  • Economic Moat: Competitive advantage sustainability

Scoring System

  • 90-100: Excellent fundamentals, strong buy candidates
  • 80-89: Good fundamentals, moderate buy
  • 70-79: Average fundamentals, selective buying
  • Below 70: Weak fundamentals, avoid or hold

Buy/Sell Timing Indicators

Entry Criteria

  • Stock trading below fair value with strong fundamentals
  • Positive price momentum and bullish trend
  • Support level holding with increasing volume
  • Sector tailwinds and favorable market conditions

Exit Strategy

  • Target price achievement (35-45% returns for strong buys)
  • Fundamental deterioration in key metrics
  • Stop loss at 15% below entry price
  • Time-based exit after 2-3 years if targets not met

Risk Management Guidelines

Portfolio Allocation

  • Strong Buy stocks: Maximum 20% per stock
  • Moderate Buy stocks: Maximum 10% per stock
  • Diversification across 8-12 sectors
  • Maximum 60% equity allocation for conservative investors

Risk Categories

  • Low Risk: Large-cap, established companies with consistent performance
  • Medium Risk: Mid-cap companies with growth potential
  • High Risk: Small-cap or volatile sectors requiring careful monitoring
